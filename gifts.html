gifts

4/14/23

as i sip on my tea and write this, i can't help but notice how there's a piece of you in everything tonight


the mug i'm drinking from, that you painted for me at that pottery store right before you moved into college freshman year

i still remember picking up the mugs and driving down to spend the night because your roommate left and you felt alone

i picked the mug on purpose tonight; everything tastes better in it


the pajama pants i'm wearing are from that out of the way trip to the gas station after you moved out

you have a matching pair, but i doubt you still wear them

these are still the comfiest pants i own; i remember when i used to wear them damn near everywhere


the shirt advertising an fake irish pub in orlando

it had been pissing rain and i got soaked, and your dad bought me the shirt even though i insisted i was fine

i was so scared to be so far away from you for the whole summer, but we made it work pretty well


the ring that you gave me for our year and a half

i really shouldn't be wearing it, but i'm hoping that it works

i'm praying that we find our way back into each others arms


but if we don't, i won't get rid of your gifts to me

i don't think i ever could
